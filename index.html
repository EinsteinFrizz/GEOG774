Aim

The aim of this project was to investigate the directional aspect of how waves propagate across shore platforms, with a particular focus on infragravity waves.
The topic arose from an identified gap in the literature on waves on shore platforms, where the vast majority of studies used transects to collect data, thereby focusing on only one dimension of wave propagation.

Location

This study was undertaken at Tatapouri shore platform, located approximately 10 kilometres east of Gisborne, New Zealand.
Tatapouri is on the western coast of the North Island, therefore having a relatively low-energy wave environment. The area is microtidal (< 2 metres tidal range), and the geology is primarily sandstone and limestone.

Tatapouri shore platform is around 250 - 300 metres wide, measured from the cliff toe to the seaward edge of the platform. It has an average elevation of 0.7 metres below sea level.
