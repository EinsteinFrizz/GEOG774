Results

The following graph (also shown in Method ) shows an example of the data that was analysed. The data is shown for three out of the six sensors only, as this waveform was not able to be identified on the other three sensors.

Since these waveforms have been corrected for time delay and normalised for height, the relationship of their similarity should be linear, meaning an R2 value is an adequate measure of correlation.

Ideally, if the waveforms are identical, all of the points on the above graphs would fall exactly on the line y = x.
The gradient of these lines shows temporal stretching due to slowing down of the waves, and the scater of the points, measured by the R2 values, indicates how similar the waveforms are between the two sensors being compared in each graph.
The amount of scatter in these plots is suprising, and the R2 values are low, meaning there is a relatively weak match between the waveforms shown above.
